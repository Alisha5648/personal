countries in which located forest

Russia, Brazil, Canada, the United States, and China collectively hold over half of the world's forests. Additionally, countries like Suriname, Guyana, Micronesia, and Gabon have a very high proportion of their land covered by forests, with over 90%.

forest of Russia

Russia boasts a vast and diverse forest ecosystem, holding more than one-fifth of the world's forest area. The dominant forest type is the boreal forest, also known as the taiga, which is the largest forested region on Earth.

forest of Brazil

The Amazon rainforest, predominantly located in Brazil, is the world's largest tropical rainforest and one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth. It covers a vast area of approximately 60% within Brazil, with the remainder extending into other South American countries

forest of canada

Canada boasts vast and diverse forests, covering nearly 40% of its landmass with 362 million hectares. These forests are a vital part of the Canadian landscape, supporting a wide range of ecological, social, and economic benefits. Canada's forests are also crucial for the global environment, particularly the boreal forest, which is one of the largest intact forests on Earth.
